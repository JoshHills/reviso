Going the extra mile...

If you're the kind of boffin who likes to do some extra reading around the subject, or you've gobbled up all of the delicious content we have to offer and are craving something more, then great! Below, I have compiled a list of links pointing to extra resources, packed with information or functionality to help solidify your knowledge on the topics covered here. For each, I've provided a helpful description and why they might be useful, and they'll allow you to spread your newly-developed wings. Interestingly, I used every single one to make this website. Fly free!

W3Schools

is a behemoth when it comes to tech-tutorials! Step-by-step interactive walkthroughs explaining the documentation of every web-oriented language you could dream of. It's like this website, but way better!

Codecademy

is a very involved tutorial website that aims to take you on a logical journey as you slowly improve and apply your skills. Very well designed, this website has helped revive the careers of computer-novices world-wide.

Stack Network

is a hub of forums dedicated to anything and everything related to programming- ask and answer questions! This is the one-stop place to go to connect with real-world developers, and recieve around the clock help from a diverse, knowledgable community.

Programming 'Sub-Reddits'

are a large collection of sub-divided communities vying to share discussion, news and support towards a range of subjects. You can dabble here for interesting fringe stories, updates, and casual discussion with similarly-minded sociopaths!

JSFiddle

is an online IDE in which you can simultaneously provide and poke at the Javascript, CSS and HTML of an encapsulated website- it's perfect for tinkering with ideas or testing selective elements of your website, and provides native support for any plugin.

GitHub

is a collaborative application that allows teams of people to contribute to the same project world-wide. Developers often dump their work here, as it serves as an online portfolio that you can extract and use elements from, provided you do so lawfully. It's worth looking into!

Brackets

is progressive text-editor developed by 'Adobe' which offers lots of incredibly promising features over other alternatives such as plugin-support, and is a generally sleek method of organising your file structure and hacking away at some code.
